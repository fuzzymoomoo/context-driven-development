# Glossary

## Context

Structured information that governs outcome. Context includes the facts, constraints, references, conventions, and signals that shape what should happen next.

## Context Bundle

The bounded set of context assembled for a specific stage or task.

## Context Contract

The formal definition of what context a stage requires and what shape of output is considered valid.

## Context Sufficiency

Whether the available context is good enough to justify execution of a stage.

## Context Mesh

The network of linked contexts that influence one another across strategic, operational, execution, and reflective concerns.

## Stage

A unit of work with defined context requirements, expected outputs, and validation rules.

## Stage Mode

A typed specialization of a stage. For example, planning may have different modes for new systems, features, bugfixes, or migrations.

## Plan

A validated semantic response to one or more contexts. Plans interpret context, define scope, and recommend next actions or stages.

## Task

An executable unit emitted from a validated plan. Tasks are operational, not the primary source of truth.

## Output

The result of execution, such as a specification, design, code change, test set, decision, or release artifact.

## Validation

The process of checking whether an output is acceptable before it becomes future truth.

## Provenance

The record of what context, constraints, actors, and validations shaped a result.

## Managed Surface

The governed part of a system where structure, patterns, and generation rules are explicit and reusable.

## Unmanaged Surface

The bounded custom area where targeted flexibility is allowed outside strongly managed generation paths.

## Generalization

The governed extraction of repeated success back into reusable capability such as templates, validators, conventions, or stronger defaults.

## View

A projection over the underlying contexts, plans, tasks, and outputs for a particular audience or purpose.
